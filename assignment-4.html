Logline
A very emptional, intimate conversation on identity crisis.
Synopsis

After spending 17 years growing up in Saudi Arabia, a young student returns to his Moroccan birthplace for college, turning the camera on himself to navigate the jarring clash of cultures and piece together what it truly means to be home.

The Journey

Making this documentary was hard because the topic is very personal to me. But I really wanted people to see the struggles that others like me face, and how those struggles are usually ignored. The hardest part was the editing. I just couldn't sit and watch an hour of myself crying and being angry. In the end, I had to ask a classmate to edit the film for me. This process taught me that showing your real self is scary but essential. I understood that carrying my whole story alone wasn't the only way, and reaching out to a classmate for help was one of the hardest things I had asked someone as i left myself in a very vulnerable position where they get to see my pure emotions for an hour or so, this became a major part of my growth.
